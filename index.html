Walter Johnson

"The Big Train"

The Life of Walter Johnson

  • 1887 - Born in Humboldt, Kansas on November 6.
  • 1907 - Makes MLB debut for the Washington Senators on August 2.
  • 1910 - has breakout year, posting 25 wins, 313 strikeouts and a 1.36 ERA.
  • 1924 - Helps lead Washington Senators to World Series, winning World Series MVP in process
  • 1927 - Retires after 1927 MLB season.
  • 1928-1935 - tenure as a manager with 2 different teams (Washington Senators and Cleveland Indians).
  • 1940 - Runs for Congress as Republican in 1940 in Maryland's 6th District.
  • 1946 - Walter Johnson dies of brain tumor in 1946 on December 10.

Awards & Accolades

  • One of 5 players inducted into the inaugural class of Baseball Hall of Fame in 1936.(Babe Ruth, Honus Wagner, Ty Cobb, Christy Mathewson are the other four.)
  • 2-time AL MVP (1913, 1924)
  • 417 career wins (2nd all-time)
  • 1.14 season ERA in 1914 (4th lowest single-season ERA all-time at time, 6th lowest all-time now)
  • 1924 World Series MVP
  • Career-high 36 wins in 1913
  • 12-time AL strikeouts leader
  • 110 career shutouts (all-time leader)
  • 3-time pitching Triple Crown winner (1913, 1918, 1924 - led league in ERA, strikeouts, and wins in same year)
  • 3,508 career strikeouts (an MLB record that stood for 50+ years)
